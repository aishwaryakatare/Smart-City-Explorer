Bannerghatta Zoo

Bannerghatta Biological Park is located 22 km south of Bangalore, India. It is a well-known park for its zoo, safari, butterfly park, and rescue center. The park is home to a variety of species of animals, birds, and reptiles. Some of the popular animals found here include tigers, lions, bears, and elephants.

The zoo is part of the Bannerghatta National Park, which also has a safari experience. Visitors can enjoy the wildlife safari, which offers an opportunity to see animals in a more natural habitat.

Established in 1971, the park was later turned into a zoo and a biological park in 2002. The park serves as a great spot for eco-tourism, conservation education, and offers a delightful experience for wildlife enthusiasts and families alike.

The zoo also plays an important role in breeding and preserving endangered species, and it provides an excellent opportunity for visitors to learn about wildlife conservation efforts.

How to reach:

By Air

Bannerghatta Zoo is approximately 45 km from Kempegowda International Airport. Tourists can take a taxi or private transport from the airport to the zoo.

By Train

The nearest railway station is Bangalore City Railway Station, which is around 25 km from the zoo. From the railway station, taxis and buses are available to reach the zoo.

By Road

The zoo is easily accessible by road from different parts of Bangalore. Visitors can take a cab, private vehicle, or use public transport to reach the zoo from major parts of the city.
